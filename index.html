About GACL

Ghana Airports Company Limited (GACL) was incorporated on 17 January 2006 and began operations on 1 January 2007. It was established following the enactment of the Civil Aviation Act, 2004 (Act 678), which decoupled airport management from the Ghana Civil Aviation Authority (GCAA) — separating the regulatory function from airport operations. GACL is a limited liability company wholly owned by the Government of Ghana and operates under the supervision of the Ministry of Transport.

GACL is responsible for the planning, development, management, operations, and maintenance of all airports in Ghana. Its flagship facility, Accra International Airport (AIA) in Accra, is the country's main international gateway and serves as a regional hub connecting West Africa to the rest of the world. In 2018, GACL opened the state-of-the-art Terminal 3 at AIA, significantly boosting capacity and passenger experience.

In addition to AIA, GACL manages five domestic airports located in Kumasi, Tamale, Sunyani, Ho, and Wa, providing vital air connectivity to the regions of Ghana. The company is also responsible for ensuring all facilities comply with the safety and security standards of the International Civil Aviation Organisation (ICAO) and the Airports Council International (ACI).
